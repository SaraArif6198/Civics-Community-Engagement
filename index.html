Story One · Meal Initiative

Not Charity —
Shared Humanity

When fuel prices rose sharply, daily life became harder for minimum-wage workers — those who maintain dignity and don't ask for help. We decided to respond quietly.

22 meal boxes every 14 days, home-cooked, packed fresh, distributed with care and without fanfare.

A rotational system — each cycle, a different member's home cooks. Fairness built into the structure itself.

"Not a photo op. Just two people acknowledging each other's humanity."

Story Two · Plantation Drive

Roots in the
Neighbourhood

The area around our local mosque sat bare and sun-baked. No shade, no greenery — just open ground people crossed quickly. Someone said half-jokingly: "We should plant a tree." The joke became a plan.

We planted 7–8 trees including fast-growing shade varieties, added flower beds bordered with white stone, and arranged potted roses and marigolds.

All five members contributed — from arranging plants and resources to physically planting and maintaining them.

"A planted tree doesn't give shade immediately. But it's a promise — that someone thought about tomorrow."
